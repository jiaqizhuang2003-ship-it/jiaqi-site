An agent without an interface is just a process you hope is doing the right thing. The work may be intelligent, but the relationship is brittle.

Good agent interfaces expose intent, uncertainty, and state. They do not need to show every token or every internal branch. They do need to show enough structure for a person to know when to trust, when to wait, and when to intervene.

The next decade of AI products will be shaped less by chat boxes and more by control surfaces. The quiet parts will matter: review queues, audit trails, memory editors, and handoff points that fit existing teams.

The best interface may feel almost boring. That is often the sign that the system has found the right amount of power.
